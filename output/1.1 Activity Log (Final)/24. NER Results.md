### **Chart 3: Score Gap Analysis** (`03_score_gap_analysis.png`)


This shows the gap between 1st and 2nd highest scoring disease per paper.



Insight:


- **Single-disease papers have LARGER gaps** (median ~50-80 points)
- **Multi-disease papers have SMALLER gaps** (median ~20-30 points)

This proves that single-disease papers are genuinely focused on one topic, not artificially classified. The gap validates that your threshold approach is correctly identifying papers where one disease dominates.

### **Chart 4: Witness Impact** (`04_witness_impact.png`)


Shows how NER model agreement affects scoring.



Key findings:


- **95.4%** of PRIMARY diseases have **3 witnesses** (all models agree)
- Only **0.2%** have just 1 witness

Insight: The witness merging step is working excellently. When BERN2 finds a disease, GLiNER and PubMedBERT almost always confirm it. This gives high confidence that extracted diseases are real entities, not noise.
