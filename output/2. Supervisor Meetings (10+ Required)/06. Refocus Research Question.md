# Refocus Research Question


## Content


## Summary

- Meeting reviewed current research proposal scope – identified as too broad.
- Advised to narrow focus by introducing a **control group** for hypothesis testing.
- Agreed on using **20–30 high-impact UK Biobank journal papers** specifically focused on **feature selection for modelling or machine learning in disease prediction**.
- Shift from direct Biobank–PubMed comparison to a more controlled, rigorous approach.
- Emphasis on ensuring data-driven selection of papers rather than relying on primitive keyword searches.

---


## Key Discussion Points

- Scope refinement critical for depth and scientific validity.
- Importance of control group: allows structured comparison and stronger conclusions.
- Keyword-based searches deemed insufficient for identifying target papers.
- Proposed use of **vector database + semantic search** for accurate, context-based retrieval.
- Avoiding common approach of simple Boolean searches on PubMed/Google Scholar, which is non-rigorous.

---


## Action Points

1. Identify and collect **20–30 target UK Biobank papers** meeting the specified criteria.
2. Design and implement **vector database** for semantic search.
3. Define feature selection/machine learning focus keywords and ontology for search optimisation.
4. Document selection methodology for reproducibility and scientific rigor.

---


## Next Steps

- Build and test semantic search pipeline to retrieve and rank candidate papers.
- Validate selected papers against agreed inclusion criteria.
